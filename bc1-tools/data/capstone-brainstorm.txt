Torbett Capstone Idea:
I want to take my capstone product and roll it into a Practicum/Thesis later in Fall '26.
As a former pentester looking to get back into industry, there is a lot of BUZZ around leveraging AI/Agents 
in pentesting activities. There is some -- albeit sparce -- academic research into this niche. Most activity
comes from industry through open source repos on Github or paid products.
Additionally, these industry products and academic research almost exclusively focus on Web Application 
security/pentesting/bug bounty hunting. When they do discuss networks they leave out Active Directory 
environments, which every Fortune 500 company uses: AD is the backbone and work horse of corporate networks.
Identifying this GAP in research and industry tools is something I want to exploit for my research and 
class activity. 
My aim is to leverage existing work on Agent Pentesting capability and develop a solution for AD 
recon/enumeration. The Recon/Enum phase of pentesting is consistenly viewed as a constant loop of scanning,
parsing, and taking further action against the results. This phase is ripe for AI Agent augmentation.
Tooling exists for conducting these activities, specifically a tool called "BloodHound".
Further, testing environments exist for AD Pentest training, namely Game of Active Directory (GOAD).
These lay the groundwork for my Graduate level Capstone project I want to develop into a larger project 
to fulfill my Graduate degree.
